Analyzing the factors

  1. Factors that affect resale price

Correlation matrix



Floor area has the highest correlation to resale price. Interestingly, mall distance, MRT distance and school distance have a weak negative correlation. Meaning the further away they are, the higher the resale price.



Scatter plot of factor against price

The issue with solely depending on a correlation matrix is that it does not account for anscombe’s quartet. Hence, the need for the scatter plot as shown on the screen. The graph shows every entry in our dataset, the distance to the nearest factor and its respective hdb resale price



Next we tried to see if the announcement and opening dates of MRT lines affect resale prices of flats near to the lines. But there isn't a clear trend that it does and the change in prices may be due to external factors instead e.g. sharp increase in 2020 could be attributed to the pandemic.

How does MRT opening and announcent dates affect price


Average price of factors based on distance

As there is no correct method to identify the number of bins to use, hence we decided to follow MOE’s guidelines where students staying within 1km of a school, gets priority to enter that given school. What is interesting, however, is that hawkers and parks show the reverse relationship, where the price becomes higher as the amenity gets further. This could possibly be due to more pests and insects respectively for hdb units close to these two amenities.
